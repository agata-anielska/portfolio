Dance school "Eat & Dance"

The subject of the project is a building serving as a dance school with a restaurant on Partyzantów Street in Wrzeszcz. The main design goal was to create a gateway passage, which is strictly defined in the local zoning plan (MPZP). The passage is three stories high and approximately 8 meters wide. The structure consists of two connected parts that are offset from each other. On the southern elevation, white shutters were designed to fully cover the building's glass facades. This was intended to protect against sunlight and also to provide privacy for the dancers practicing in the halls. On the northern side, the glass facades make up a significantly smaller portion of the elevation and do not have shutters. From this side, there is a view of de Gaulle Park, located near the project site. The building's functional program includes dance and workshop rooms with support facilities, a restaurant, and a lobby. Two entrances to the building were designed: one in the part of the structure extending more to the south, serving as the entrance to the restaurant, and the other in the part extending more to the north, serving as the entrance to the dance school. The building's floors vary in height. The support facilities occupy one floor, while the halls occupy two floors, allowing dancers to use the spaces freely.
