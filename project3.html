Social Services Center in Sopot

The project concerns a building serving as an Eco-Recycling Center in Sopot, located on Haffner Street, at the border of the Kamienny Potok and Dolny Sopot districts. The idea for the project arose from the growing problem of improperly recycled waste and the desire to create a space that would serve the residents for recreational purposes. The proposed development consists of three buildings. The first, located at the western edge of the site, is intended to function as a sorting facility where items brought for recycling would be processed and then transferred to the main building, which is the recycling center itself, located at the northern boundary. At the eastern edge of the site, a research institute is planned, where materials would be studied for recycling and methods to improve recycling efficiency would be developed. The name “Heart” comes from both a literal and metaphorical reference. When viewed from above, the ground floor plan of the building resembles a heart. The building is also the heart of the entire complex, as it will primarily attract people from the neighboring buildings and residents of Sopot visiting the site. The structure itself combines three elements: solid walls along the northern boundary of the site and cascading terraces with organic shapes on the southern side, which reflect the natural environment extending to the south of the site. Creating a space for integration and relaxation for visitors was also crucial to the concept. Hence, the idea to complement the structure with an external staircase that allows for the observation of the surroundings from a certain height.
